# Artifact Learnings

## What did not work

The earlier version looked like a compact workbench, but it did not explain why the workbench existed. A first-time reader saw controls, scores, and blockers before they understood the study question.

The page also over-optimized for fitting everything into one desktop viewport. That made the artifact dense, but not persuasive. The right goal is a readable study with an interactive/code proof embedded inside it.

## What changed

The artifact now follows a clearer study narrative:

1. Research question: when is an AI workflow actually ready for a customer pilot?
2. Hypothesis: pilot readiness depends on workflow reality, contract checks, evaluation, and product signal.
3. Code experiment: typed synthetic workflows are scored, blocked, repaired, and converted into a recommendation.
4. Result: the output is a controlled pilot decision with a blocker, repair path, and roadmap signal.
5. Learnings: the value is in the decision record around the AI output, not just the output itself.

## Design rule for this project

Do not present the interface before presenting the investigation. The reader needs to understand what was studied, why the framework matters, and what the code proves before the controls are useful.
